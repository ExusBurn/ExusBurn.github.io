Distributed Krylov Quantum Diagonalization

2025–26

Final project · Introduction to Scalable Systems (Parallel Programming) · IISc CDS

Built a fully distributed, GPU-accelerated system for quantum ground-state energy estimation via the KQD algorithm, implementing hand-written CUDA kernels (fused Pauli gates, shared-memory reductions, coalesced access) and CUDA-aware MPI for multi-GPU statevector partitioning, alongside a CPU reference in OpenMP + MPI. This project deepened my understanding of low-level GPU architecture, the cost of abstraction in scientific frameworks, and communication-overlap strategies in distributed systems. Achieved 6.2× speedup over NVIDIA CUDA-Q at 23 qubits with near-ideal strong scaling across 3 GPUs. →

Quantum Solution of the Heat Equation (LCHS)

2025–26

Final project · Numerical Solution of Differential Equations · IISc CDS

Implemented the Linear Combination of Hamiltonian Simulation (LCHS) algorithm in Qiskit to solve the 1D diffusion equation, using 4 system qubits, 6 ancilla qubits, and 64 Gauss–Legendre quadrature nodes as Hamiltonian simulation weights within a V-PREPARE / SELECT / UNPREP circuit structure, validated against classical FTCS finite difference and analysed spectrally across Fourier modes. This taught me how quantum algorithms map to linear operator problems and the interplay between quadrature accuracy and circuit fidelity. LCHS accurately reproduced low-frequency modes; high-frequency error increased as expected due to quadrature truncation. →
