About Me

I am a graduate student in the Department of Computational and Data Sciences (CDS) at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore. My work sits at the intersection of quantum computing, high-performance numerical simulation, and machine learning — areas where theoretical rigour and computational performance must go hand in hand.

I am broadly passionate about developing algorithms that push the limits of what computers — classical or quantum — can efficiently simulate and learn from. My current interests span quantum algorithms for differential equations, GPU-accelerated quantum simulation, and deep generative models.

Prior to IISc I worked on robotics research, building vision-guided manipulation systems for robotic arms — from blind pick-and-place pipelines to camera-driven dynamic grasping with autonomous vehicles.

Research Interests

  • Quantum Computing & Simulation — Quantum algorithms for linear differential equations (LCHS), Krylov subspace methods for Hamiltonian diagonalization, and quantum circuit design on real hardware via Qiskit and NVIDIA CUDA-Q.
  • High-Performance Computing — GPU kernel development in CUDA, distributed computing with MPI, and shared-memory parallelism via OpenMP. Interested in the co-design of algorithms and hardware for scientific computing workloads.
  • Machine Learning & Deep Learning — From classical statistical learning (SVMs, GMMs, logistic regression) to modern architectures — Transformers, Vision Transformers, GANs, VAEs, and self-supervised contrastive learning. Particular interest in generative models and their theoretical foundations.
  • Numerical Methods — Finite difference and finite element methods for PDEs, spectral methods, and the emerging intersection of quantum algorithms with numerical analysis.
  • Robotics & Perception — Vision-guided manipulation, real-time object tracking, and sensor fusion for autonomous robotic systems.

Distributed Krylov Quantum Diagonalization

[GitHub]

Parallel Programming — Final Project  ·  IISc CDS, 2026

This project implements a fully distributed, GPU-accelerated Krylov Quantum Diagonalization (KQD) algorithm for solving the time-independent Schrödinger equation on the Heisenberg XXX spin chain. The implementation achieves a 6.2× speedup over the NVIDIA CUDA-Q baseline at 23 qubits, with near-ideal strong scaling across 3 GPUs using CUDA-aware MPI.

Key techniques include hand-written CUDA kernels for direct statevector manipulation, fused two-qubit gate operations, shared-memory tree reductions, and statevector reuse to avoid redundant memory transfers. An OpenMP/MPI CPU port is also provided for comparison. The work benchmarks against NVIDIA's high-level CUDA-Q framework and demonstrates the performance gains achievable through careful low-level GPU programming.

CUDA  ·  C++  ·  MPI  ·  OpenMP  ·  NVIDIA CUDA-Q

Quantum Solution of the 1D Heat Equation via LCHS

[GitHub]

Numerical Solutions of Differential Equations — Final Project  ·  IISc CDS, 2026

An implementation of the Linear Combination of Hamiltonian Simulation (LCHS) algorithm in Qiskit to solve the one-dimensional diffusion equation ∂u/∂t = α(t) ∂²u/∂x² with periodic boundary conditions on [0, 2π]. The spatial domain is discretized into 16 grid points, and the time integral is approximated using 64 Gauss–Legendre quadrature nodes, each corresponding to a controlled unitary quantum circuit block.

The quantum solution is benchmarked against the classical Forward-Time Centered-Space (FTCS) finite difference method. Results are presented via spatial profiles, amplitude decay curves, and Fourier spectral analysis, providing a detailed picture of where quantum and classical solutions agree and diverge.

Qiskit  ·  Python  ·  NumPy  ·  SciPy  ·  Quantum Algorithms

Pattern Recognition and Neural Networks — Assignments

[GitHub]

PRNN Course  ·  IISc CDS, 2026

A three-part deep-dive into the theory and practice of machine learning and neural networks.

  • Assignment 1 — Classical ML from scratch: Ordinary least squares regression, support vector machines, logistic regression, Gaussian mixture models, and Naïve Bayes — all implemented directly in NumPy/SciPy without high-level ML libraries. Includes a vectorised KNN implementation achieving a 94× speedup over a naive loop.
  • Assignment 2 — Deep learning with PyTorch: MLPs, CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, GRUs, Transformers, and Vision Transformers on real-world datasets including Delhi air quality time-series, PlantVillage crop disease imagery, and APTOS retinal fundus images. Includes vanishing gradient demonstrations and FID score computation.
  • Assignment 3 — Generative models and RL: Ensemble methods (Random Forests, Gradient Boosting), Generative Adversarial Networks, Variational Autoencoders, SimCLR self-supervised contrastive learning, and the REINFORCE policy gradient algorithm.
