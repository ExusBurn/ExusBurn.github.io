About Me

I grew up with a fascination for the question at the heart of computer science: what can be efficiently computed? That question led me naturally toward its most radical frontier — quantum computing — where the rules of what is "efficiently computable" are being rewritten. But I have always believed that theory without implementation is incomplete, which is why my work is equally grounded in high-performance systems programming, GPU architectures, and the careful engineering required to make algorithms run fast on real hardware.

At IISc, I am part of the Department of Computational and Data Sciences, a uniquely interdisciplinary department that bridges mathematics, computer science, and domain-specific scientific computing. The curriculum is designed to develop researchers who are equally comfortable with theoretical analysis and systems-level implementation — an ethos that deeply resonates with how I approach problems.

My graduate coursework has spanned pattern recognition and neural networks (covering the full arc from classical statistical learning to modern Transformers and generative models), parallel programming (CUDA, MPI, OpenMP), numerical solutions of differential equations (finite difference, finite element, and spectral methods), and natural language processing. Each course has produced a substantial project or set of assignments, all of which are documented and publicly available on GitHub.

Before IISc, I was involved in robotics research, working on a 7-Degrees of Freedom robotic arm system that integrated computer vision, YOLOv3-based object detection, and Visual SLAM for autonomous indoor navigation. That work led to a publication at the 9th International Conference on Computer Vision and Image Processing (CVIP 2024), co-authored with collaborators from IIST Thiruvananthapuram and BITS Goa.

Most recently I interned at Mastercard, working on sequential recommendation in a continual learning setting — Transformer-based next-item prediction over non-stationary interaction streams, and the knowledge-distillation machinery that lets a compact, servable model keep pace with a data stream without forgetting what it already knew.

Outside of research, I run a YouTube channel — Super Eternal Sunshine — and have also documented my GATE preparation journey for the benefit of future aspirants hoping to enter IISc or other top research institutions in India.

Research Interests

My interests span several connected areas within computational science. What unites them is a focus on algorithms that are both theoretically principled and practically performant.

  • Quantum Algorithms & Simulation — I am interested in quantum algorithms that offer provable advantages over classical methods, particularly for problems in linear algebra and differential equations. My work has touched on the Linear Combination of Hamiltonian Simulation (LCHS) framework for solving linear ODEs/PDEs, and Krylov subspace methods for quantum diagonalization. I work with both Qiskit (IBM) and NVIDIA's CUDA-Q framework.
  • High-Performance & GPU Computing — I care deeply about the systems layer — writing CUDA kernels by hand, optimising memory access patterns, and using MPI for distributed GPU computation. My KQD project demonstrates what is possible when algorithms are co-designed with hardware: careful statevector manipulation, fused gate operations, and shared-memory reductions yield 6.2× speedups over a high-level framework baseline.
  • Machine Learning & Deep Learning — From the mathematical foundations of SVMs and Gaussian mixture models to the architectural innovations of Vision Transformers, GANs, and VAEs — I am interested in understanding why these models work and building them from first principles. I have particular interest in generative modelling and self-supervised learning.
  • Numerical Methods for PDEs — Finite difference and finite element methods, Krylov solvers, spectral methods, and the emerging area of quantum-accelerated numerical analysis. I am especially interested in the question of where quantum algorithms genuinely help in solving PDEs and where they do not.
  • Robotics & Perception — Vision-guided robotic manipulation, SLAM, real-time object tracking, and sensor fusion. My prior research demonstrated a complete pipeline from camera input to physical grasping, validated at a 94% success rate in experimental settings.
