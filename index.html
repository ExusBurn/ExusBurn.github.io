About

I'm a graduate student in the Department of Computational and Data Sciences at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore. My work sits at the intersection of quantum algorithms, GPU-accelerated computing, and modern machine learning.

I enjoy building things from scratch — hand-written CUDA kernels for quantum simulation, classical ML algorithms in NumPy, and vision-guided robotic manipulation systems. I care about understanding why things work, not just that they do.

Outside coursework I've worked on robotics research involving robotic arm manipulation, object tracking, and autonomous pick-and-place pipelines.

Quantum · HPC ⚛

Distributed Krylov Quantum Diagonalization

GPU-accelerated KQD algorithm for solving the time-independent Schrödinger equation on the Heisenberg XXX spin chain. Achieves 6.2× speedup over NVIDIA CUDA-Q baseline at 23 qubits with near-ideal strong scaling across 3 GPUs.

Quantum Solution of the Heat Equation

Implements the Linear Combination of Hamiltonian Simulation (LCHS) algorithm in Qiskit to solve the 1D diffusion equation. Benchmarks quantum circuits against classical FTCS finite difference with spectral analysis.
